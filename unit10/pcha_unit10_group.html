1. Graph the given function on the specified interval. Find all critical points. Identify any points where there is a local min/max, and verify with a derivative test. Identify the absolute max and min. If either fails to exist, state the condition of the Extreme Value Theorem that is not satisfied.

  1. \(g(x) = x^2-9\) on \([-3,3]\)
  1. \(h(x) = \ln x\) on \((0,2]\)
  1. \(m(x) = \tan x\) on \([0, \pi]\)
  1. \(n(x) = \sin x\) on \((0, \pi)\)

2. For each of the given functions find all antiderivatives.

  1. \(p'(x) = x^4\)
  1. \(q'(x) = \cos 2x\)
  1. \(r'(x) = \dfrac{1}{x}\)
  1. \(s'(x) = \dfrac{1}{x^2}\)
  1. \(t'(x) = e^{\frac{x}{3}}\)

3. For each of the following functions: sketch the graph, then find all local extrema and verify with a derivative test.

  1. \(f(x) = (x+3)^4\).
  1. \(g(x) = x^3-9x\).
  1. \(h(x) = 3 + 3 \sin 2x\). You may restrict your attention to the first period of the function. But as an extra challenge, identify all local extrema (not just the first period), including derivative tests to show which are minima and which are maxima.

4. Consider the following rectangle inscribed in a circle of radius 5. Note that the perimeter of the rectangle changes as the angle \(\theta\) changes.

  1. Write an equation for the perimeter \(P(\theta)\) of the rectangle as a function of \(\theta\). Challenge: draw a sketch of the graph of \(P(\theta)\) on \([0, \frac{\pi}{2}]\) by hand, and then check with graphing software.
  1. Find the absolute min and max of the perimeter, for \(\theta\) in \([0, \frac{\pi}{2}]\). Why must there be an absolute minimum and maximum?
